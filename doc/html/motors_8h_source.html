30 /***
31  * With a set of four NiMH AA cells loaded up, the mouse can just about do
32  * speed 1000 with microstepping set to half steps (MICROSTEP_2)
33  *
34  * Safer is half the speed with quarter stepping (MICROSTEP_4)
35  *
36  * With the acceleration table supplied, the maximum step frequency is 2.5kHz
37  *
38  * For this mouse, that corresponds to a top speed, using quarter steps, of about
39  *
40  * 30*3.14mm * 2500 /(200*4) = 295mm/s
41  *
42  * Not very quick.
43  *
44  * Take care before trying for higher frequencies since the ISRs will hve to run
45  * 5000 times per second (2 wheels) along with the 4kHz sensor interrupt. The
46  * sensor interrupt can be streamlined by directly talking to the ADC rather than
47  * use the Arduino calls.
48  *
49  * Potentially, nearly 1200mm/s could be achieved at this pulse frequency if the
50  * motor can be carefully accelerated while changing microstep mode.
51  *
52  * Alternatively, less weight would reduce the load seen by the motors or a
53  * higher voltage could be applied to the motor drivers up to 10.8 Volts for the
54  * DRV8834. Really, to get best performance, voltages of twice that will most
55  * likely be needed but the DRV8834 could not cope.
56  *
57  * Live with the lower speed. Experiment with different microstep modes. This is
58  * a training platform, not an international contest entry.
59  *
60  */
